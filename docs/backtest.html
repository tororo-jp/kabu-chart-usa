📖 Backtest Methodology & How to Read the Results ▼

🔮 What Are We Testing?

Every signal detected by the daily scanner is simulated using a simple rule: buy at the next open, sell when the target or stop-loss is hit.

  • All signals from past scan archives are loaded
  • Filtered to match real entry criteria (see below)
  • Win/loss determined against actual OHLCV data from Yahoo Finance

🎯 Entry Selection Logic (Filters)

Rather than testing every signal, entries are filtered to match realistic trading criteria.

  • Score ≥ 60: Only stocks with solid technical backing
  • Prob ≥ 55%: Model judges the setup as favorable
  • RR ≥ 1.5: Expected reward at least 1.5× the risk
  • Max 5/day: Limits daily concentration risk
  • Cooldown 5 days: Skips repeat signals on the same stock

On each day, the top 5 signals by score are selected.

⚖️ Win/Loss Rules

After entry, the outcome is determined by whichever price is reached first:

  • ✓ Target Hit: High touches the target price first
  • ✗ Stopped Out: Low touches the stop-loss first
  • ⌛ Expired: Neither hit within weeks_max → exit at period-end close
  • 📈 Open: Result not yet determined

"Expired" trades are close to a loss but may finish slightly positive.

💰 Capital Simulation

$10,000 starting capital, fixed 1% risk per trade.

  • Risk amount = Capital × 1%
  • Shares = Risk amount ÷ (Entry − Stop price)
  • Example: Capital $10,000, stop width $2.00 → buy 50 shares

Slippage, commissions, and taxes are not included. Actual performance may be 1–2% worse.

📊 Reading the Metrics

Win Rate Percentage of completed trades that hit the target. Target: ≥ 55% stable, ≥ 60% excellent
Profit Factor (PF) Total profit ÷ total loss. PF ≤ 1.0 means long-term losses. High RR ratio allows PF > 1 even with lower win rate. Target: ≥ 1.5, ≥ 2.0 excellent
Expected Value (EV) Average expected return per trade (%).
= Win rate × avg win + loss rate × avg loss Target: ≥ 0% (positive edge)
Sharpe Ratio (annualized) Return ÷ risk (std dev), annualized. Higher = better return per unit of volatility. Target: ≥ 1.0 good, ≥ 1.5 excellent
Max Drawdown (MDD) Largest peak-to-trough decline in capital (%). High MDD makes it psychologically hard to stay in. Target: keep under 20%
Expired Trades that reached weeks_max without hitting target or stop. Too many may indicate targets are set too far. Ideal: ≤ 20% of all trades

📐 Probability Calculation

Win probability is a blend of two components:

  • Score-based probability (60% weight): 0.30 + (score/100) × 0.50. Score 60 → 60%, Score 80 → 70%.
  • Pattern win rate (40% weight): Theoretical win rate per pattern, weighted by (pattern weight × confidence). High-precision patterns like VCP/CwH are weighted more heavily.

RS is already included in the score, so it is not double-counted in the probability (only a tiny bonus for RS ≥ 90).

🔄 Using Score Component Analysis

Compare which score components differ between winning and losing trades.

  • Positive diff → component correlates with wins
  • Near zero → component may have weak predictive power
  • Negative diff → component may be counterproductive

As data accumulates, increase weights for high-diff components and reduce weights for low-diff ones to improve the model.

📏 Reading Probability Calibration

Compares the scanner's predicted probability against actual win rate.

  • Deviation ≈ 0: Model is well calibrated
  • Actual > Predicted (positive gap): Underestimating. Consider raising probability thresholds.
  • Actual < Predicted (negative gap): Overestimating. Tighten the threshold.

At least 20–30 trades per bucket are needed for statistically valid conclusions.

⌛ Model Improvement Cycle

Once backtest data accumulates, improve the model with these steps:

  • ① Score component analysis: identify high-diff components
  • ② By-pattern breakdown: find patterns where actual win rate diverges from theoretical
  • ③ Calibration: assess overall over/under-estimation
  • ④ Update weights in scorer.py and PATTERN_WIN_RATES
  • ⑤ Re-scan → accumulate data → repeat

Rough timeline: 3 months for calibration baseline, 6 months for meaningful improvement decisions.

⚠️ Backtest Limitations & Caveats
① Slippage not included: Gap-up/down at the open shifts actual entry price. Real returns may be 1–3% worse.
② Commissions & taxes excluded: US long-term gains are taxed at 0/15/20%; short-term at ordinary income rates.
③ Curve-fitting risk: Parameters optimized on historical data may not hold in the future.
④ Liquidity risk: Thinly traded stocks may not fill at the theoretical price.
⑤ Market regime dependence: Pattern effectiveness changes significantly between bull and bear markets. Use the market-environment filter.
