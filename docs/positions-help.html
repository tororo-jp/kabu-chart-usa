What Is the Position Manager?

After purchasing a stock detected by the scanner, manage all your open positions — P&L, progress, and stop-loss levels — in a single screen.

  • Enter a ticker to auto-fill target, stop-loss, and ATR from today’s scan results
  • Compare daily scan data to show P&L, score, and distance to target/stop in real time
  • Stop Advice column: automatically calculates optimal stop-raise stage from current price and ATR
  • Record exit price and date to maintain a trade history

Position data is stored in browser localStorage. It is never sent to any server. Clearing your browser cache will delete all data. There is no export feature — record important data via screenshots or notes.

Adding a Position

1
Click the “＋ Add Position” button The input form will expand.
2
Enter a ticker and press “Auto-fill from Scan” Today’s scan data will auto-fill the company name, suggested entry price, target, and stop-loss. Stocks not in the scan must be entered manually.
3
Enter your actual fill price Changing the entry price to your actual fill will auto-recalculate the target and stop-loss. Also enter the number of shares and entry date.
4
Press the “Add” button The position is added to the Open Positions table. ATR is saved at this time, so stop advice continues even if the stock later leaves the scan.

What Is Stop Advice?

Using current price and ATR (Average True Range) from daily scan data, this feature automatically calculates the optimal timing and price to raise your stop-loss.

By gradually raising the stop-loss as the price rises, you can protect profits while continuing to ride the uptrend (trailing stop).

What Is ATR (Average True Range)?

ATR is the 14-day average of the “true range,” representing each stock’s daily volatility. For example, if ATR = $2.00, the stock moves an average of $2 per day. Using ATR for stop sizing ensures a rational buffer suited to each stock’s price range and volatility.

ATR UsageMeaning
Entry − ATR×2Initial stop (survives 2 average daily swings)
EntryBreakeven (zero-risk point)
Entry + ATR×1Lock in 1 ATR of profit
Price − ATR×1.5Trailing (allows 1.5 ATR of swing while following price)

ATR Source Priority

  1. Today’s scan result (latest ATR)
  2. ATR saved at registration time (works even if stock is off-scan)
  3. Fallback: 3% of entry price (substitute when ATR is unavailable)

Stage Meanings & Actions

The Stop Advice column shows a 4-stage badge based on current conditions.

StageColorTrigger ConditionRecommended StopAction
Initial Gray Price < Entry + ATR×0.8 Entry − ATR×2 No change needed. Keep original stop-loss.
Breakeven Blue Price ≥ Entry + ATR×0.8 Entry price Raise stop to entry price (zero-risk). Worst case from here is a breakeven exit.
Lock Profit Orange Progress ≥ 50% Entry + ATR×1 Raise stop further to lock in partial profit. If hit, at least 1 ATR of profit is preserved.
Trailing Green Progress ≥ 70% Price − ATR×1.5 Follow daily to maximize profits. Raises automatically as price climbs. Update each day.

Can Stages Go Backwards?

Stages are always calculated from the current price relative to entry and target. A temporary price drop may shift a stage backward, but the stop-loss is only raised — never lowered. The “↑ Apply” button is active only when the recommended stop is higher than the current stop.

💡 Progress = (Price − Entry) ÷ (Target − Entry) × 100%.
If no target is set, only stages Initial or Breakeven are evaluated. We recommend setting a target price when registering.

Applying Stop Updates

Once you’ve reviewed the stop advice, update the stop-loss using one of these methods.

Apply to Individual Positions

Press the ↑ Apply button in the Stop Advice column to update that position’s stop-loss to the recommended price. The button appears only when the recommended price is higher than the current stop.

Apply to All Positions at Once

Press the ↑ Apply All button in the table header to bulk-update all eligible positions. This button appears only when at least one position has an available update.

⚠️ The stop-loss price in this tool is a management record only. Stop orders in your brokerage account must be updated manually. Updating the tool does NOT automatically change any brokerage orders.

Post-Update Confirmation Flow

1
Press “↑ Apply All” to update the tool’s stop-loss All eligible stops are updated to the recommended price.
2
Open your brokerage app and update your stop orders Set your brokerage stop orders to match the updated “Stop Loss” column values.
3
Confirm the changes Verify in your brokerage that the stop orders are updated correctly.

Stop-Loss Rules

Rules to Always Follow

RuleReason
Only raise stops, never lower them Lowering a stop risks unlimited losses. Respecting your stop is the foundation of systematic trading.
Execute the stop immediately when hit “Wait for a bounce” hope statistically leads to larger losses. Automate with stop orders where possible.
Set a stop order immediately after purchase Set it mechanically before emotion takes over. Forgetting exposes you to unlimited loss.
Never “wait and see” when price nears the stop Watching and hoping invalidates the stop. If no stop order is in place, set one immediately.

When Score Drops or Goes Off-Scan

If a held stock shows ‘Off-scan’ or its score drops significantly, check the following:

SituationResponse
Score drops by 10+ points Technical environment deteriorating. Tighten the stop closer to current price. Execute cleanly if stop is hit.
Score goes ‘Off-scan’ (< 30) Signal has disappeared. Even if stop hasn’t been hit, the rationale for the trade has weakened. Consider partial or full exit.
Pattern has broken down (check chart) Entry rationale is gone. Consider early exit even if stop hasn’t been triggered.

A stop-loss is not a failure — it is a planned exit. Executing stops promptly keeps capital available for the next opportunity.

Recording a Close

When you sell (close) a position, record it in the tool as well.

1
Press the “Close” button on the target position The close recording modal will appear.
2
Enter exit price and exit date Check your brokerage’s fill history and enter the exact values. Defaults to current price and today’s date.
3
Press “Save” The position moves from Open Positions to Closed Trades. The realized P&L is displayed.

💡 Closed trades can be removed with the “Delete” button. Deleted data cannot be recovered.

Daily Workflow (approx. 3 minutes)

Check the following after each trading day, once the scan has completed (typically by 5:00 PM ET).

1
Check for score-drop alerts If any held stocks appear in alerts, open this page and check their Score column.
2
Check the “Stop Updates” card If shown, press “↑ Apply All” to raise the stops.
3
Check red “To Stop” column values If any show red (within 3%), verify that your brokerage stop orders are correctly set.
4
Update brokerage stop orders Manually update your brokerage stop orders to match the stops updated in Step 2.
5
Check for off-scan or score-drop stocks Signal of deteriorating technical conditions. Tighten stops or consider early exit.

Daily Checklist (copy and use)

☐ Check score-drop alerts — are any held stocks listed? ☐ Press “↑ Apply All” if the Stop Updates card is shown ☐ Check “To Stop” red values — verify brokerage stop orders ☐ Update brokerage stop orders to match raised stops ☐ Assess any “Off-scan” or score-drop stocks and decide on action
